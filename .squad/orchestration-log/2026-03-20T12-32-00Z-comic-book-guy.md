## Task: Spatial Relationships & Stacking System Design

**Status:** ✅ COMPLETED

### Work Performed

1. **Designed five-relationship spatial model:**
   - ON (rests on surface)
   - UNDER (hidden beneath)
   - BEHIND (blocked by obstacle)
   - COVERING (conceals what's below)
   - INSIDE (in a container)

2. **Established stacking rules:**
   - Stackable surfaces declare `is_stackable_surface = true/false`
   - Weight capacity enforcement (Light/Medium/Heavy categories)
   - Size capacity validation
   - Four surface models: flat, large, stacking, container

3. **Designed hidden object discovery system:**
   - Three states: Hidden → Hinted → Revealed
   - Declarative triggers: covering_object_moves, player_searches, state_change
   - Discovery messages fire on reveal

4. **Defined movable furniture mechanics:**
   - PUSH, PULL, MOVE verbs with optional directions
   - Difficulty tiers (Easy/Moderate/Hard)
   - Movement updates all relationships atomically

5. **Extended verb system:**
   - LIFT / RAISE (temporary peek)
   - LOOK UNDER / FEEL UNDER (tactile discovery in darkness)
   - LOOK BEHIND (spatial occlusion)
   - PUSH / PULL (furniture movement)
   - MOVE (generic relocation)

**Eight Core Design Decisions:**
1. Five distinct relationships (not merged UNDER/BEHIND)
2. Covering is bi-directional
3. Weight & size capacity are hard rules
4. Hidden objects don't exist until revealed
5. Discovery triggers are declarative
6. Movement updates all relationships atomically
7. LIFT is different from MOVE
8. Darkness doesn't disable spatial verbs

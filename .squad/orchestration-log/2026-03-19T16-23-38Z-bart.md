## Assignment
Fix feel verb container contents bug. The FEEL handler was not enumerating accessible surface and container contents after on_feel text, breaking the darkness gameplay loop (matchbox not discoverable via feel).

## Outcome
✅ COMPLETE

### What Was Done
1. **Root Cause:** FEEL handler in `src/engine/verbs/init.lua` printed the `on_feel` text but skipped enumeration of accessible contents.
2. **Implementation:** Modified feel handler to enumerate after the sensory description:
   - **Surface zones:** If `obj.surfaces` exists and zone is `accessible ~= false`, list zone contents with prefix "Your fingers find {zone_name}:"
   - **Simple containers:** If `obj.container` has contents, list with prefix "Inside you feel:"
   - **Resolution:** Use `ctx.registry:get(id)` for item names, fallback to raw ID
3. **Pattern:** Matches the existing LOOK handler enumeration logic — consistent behavior across senses
4. **Tactile language:** "Your fingers find" and "Inside you feel" preserve darkness atmosphere
5. **Testing:** Verified in-game — matchbox now discoverable by feeling the nightstand drawer

### Files Modified
- `src/engine/verbs/init.lua` — ~20 lines added after line 841 (feel handler)

### Impact
- Puzzle solvability restored: players can now discover container contents via touch
- Satisfies the "progressive disclosure" design: feel around (summary) → feel {object} (detail + accessible contents)
- Respects accessibility gates: closed drawers (`accessible == false`) hide contents from touch, same as from sight

## Decision
**Decision: Feel Verb Enumerates Container/Surface Contents** (merged to `.squad/decisions.md`)

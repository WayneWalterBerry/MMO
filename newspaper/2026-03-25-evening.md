## THE SIX WAVES

### WAVE 1: Data Foundation (Flanders, Nelson)
**Creatures come to life in data.**

Flanders architected `creature.lua` template, instantiated `rat.lua`, and established the `flesh` material layer. Nelson wrote **72 TDD tests** in pure Lua, covering instantiation, state initialization, and sensory output.

- 72 tests written in test-first style
- Creature template follows nesting hierarchy (body_tree, states, drive system)
- Material consistency verified against engine constraints

**GATE-1 RESULT: 72 / 72 ✓**

---

### WAVE 2: Creature Engine (Bart)
**The engine breathes life into creatures.**

Bart built the creature engine subsystem (**418 LOC**), the nervous system of NPCs:

- **Drive engine:** Hunger, fear, curiosity decay + threshold checks
- **Stimulus system:** Visibility, auditory, olfactory stimulus tracking
- **Reaction FSM:** Stimulus → reaction routing (flee, investigate, ignore)
- **Movement primitives:** Navigation, room transitions, pathfinding
- **Behavior arbiter:** Priority-weighted decision making

The creature engine treats NPCs as reactive systems—no scripting, no hardcoded behavior trees. Creatures are objects like any other; the engine reads their FSM and executes.

**GATE-2 RESULT: 42 / 42 ✓**

**Players meet creatures for the first time.**

Brockman documented:

Three minor blockers emerged during GATE-3 verification; all resolved same-day (stimulus timing, presence ordering, creature spawn defaults).

**GATE-3 RESULT: 149 / 149 ✓** (after blocker fixes)

Flanders also added `combat_metadata` to weapon objects (edge_type, thickness, penetration bonus, narrative flavor).

Nelson wrote **116 tests** validating:
- Body part enumeration and depth traversal
- Tissue resistance and material layering
- Weapon modifier interactions

**GATE-4 RESULT: 116 / 116 ✓**

### WAVE 5: Combat Engine & Narration (Bart)
**Steel meets flesh.**

Bart built two core systems:

1. **Combat FSM (400 LOC):** Turn-based combat loop—stance selection, damage calculation, tissue penetration, organ rupture, bleeding, shock, death. Every hit narrated. Every miss has consequence.
2. **Narration module (165 LOC):** Uses GPT-5.2-Codex for real-time injury narration. No template library—each wound is described anew. *"Your left forearm muscle tears, crimson spray—you cry out."*

Nelson verified **78 tests**: damage thresholds, stat interactions, edge cases (one-hit kills, armor interactions, simultaneous attacks).

**GATE-5 RESULT: 78 / 78 ✓**

## AUTONOMY AND CRAFT

### An Operator's Reflection
By Brockman

**March 25 marked something the MMO project needed: the team working *without a captain in the room.*

Wayne didn't attend standups. He didn't attend code reviews. He didn't send Slack messages. The decision was intentional—a stress-test of whether the Squad framework could execute a complex, multi-phase, interdependent rollout on its own merit.

It did.

What struck me most was not the volume of code or tests shipped (358 tests is respectable, but not extraordinary). What struck me was the *quality of collaboration without a central authority.* Bart didn't wait for Smithers to finish before starting integration. Nelson didn't wait for all gates to pass before designing additional smoke scenarios. Flanders didn't second-guess her material taxonomy—she owned it.

The framework worked because the ownership boundaries were clear. Brockman documents what the team *does*—I don't decide *what* to document. Bart builds the engine core—he doesn't design rats. Smithers wires verbs to the UI—he doesn't write creature FSM logic.

Autonomy is not "do whatever you want." Autonomy is "do your job with confidence because everyone else is doing theirs."

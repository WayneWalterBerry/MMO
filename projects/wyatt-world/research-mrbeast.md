- Color-coded zones (red zone = danger, green zone = safe, gold = reward)
- Oversized props (giant buttons, huge timers, massive doors)

- Modular and reconfigurable — each challenge feels fresh

## 5. Catchphrases & Language

### Things Mr. Beast Says (A 10-Year-Old Would Quote)

| Phrase | When Used |
|--------|----------|
| **"Subscribe for a cookie!"** | Channel call-to-action |
| **"Let's go!"** | Start of challenge, celebration |
| **"Last one standing wins!"** | Challenge announcement |
| **"Today we're doing [crazy thing] and the winner gets [huge prize]!"** | Every video intro |
| **"I gave away..."** | Video title/tease |
| **"If you subscribe right now..."** | Engagement hook |
| **"Me being an idiot is the core part of my videos!"** | Self-deprecating humor |

### Language Style Guide (For Game Text)
- **Simple, direct sentences** — "You have 60 seconds. Grab everything you can."
- **Exclamation energy** — "The door is opening! Let's go!"
- **Big numbers** — "$500,000! $1,000,000! $5,000,000!"
- **Action verbs** — give, grab, win, survive, challenge, race
- **Inclusive "we"** — "We're doing this together!"
- **Hype tone** — Always excited, never boring

### Tone Rules for Wyatt's World
1. Keep sentences short (8–12 words)
2. Use words a 3rd grader knows
3. Exclamation marks are OK (but not every sentence)
4. Numbers should be spelled out under 100, numerals for big amounts
5. Always positive and encouraging — no harsh failure messages

## 7. Educational Angles

### Math & Money Skills
- **Counting big prizes** — "You found $10,000! That's ten thousand dollars!"
- **Addition/subtraction** — "You had $50,000. You spent $15,000 on the key. How much is left?"
- **Comparison** — "The gold bar is worth MORE than the silver coins."
- **Budgets** — "You have $100 to spend. What do you buy?"
- Opportunity cost — take the $1,000 now or risk it for $10,000?

### Reading Comprehension
- Clue-reading to solve challenges ("The note says: look under the RED button")
- Following multi-step instructions
- Vocabulary building through context (challenge, obstacle, vault, treasure)
- Cause and effect ("If you pull the lever, the door opens")

### Strategy & Decision-Making
- Risk assessment — safe choice vs. big reward
- Resource management — limited inventory, consumable items
- Sequencing — do things in the right order
- Pattern recognition — "Every time you see a gold star, good things happen"

### Teamwork & Values
- Helping NPC characters to progress
- Sharing/giving as a game mechanic (Beast Philanthropy spirit)
- Kindness-based puzzle solutions (give food to hungry NPC → they help you)
- "#TeamTrees, #TeamSeas" — environmental awareness woven into world

## Room Concept Seeds (10 Ideas)

### 1. 🏭 The Beast Warehouse (Hub Room)
**Concept:** A massive warehouse with colored doors leading to challenge rooms. MrBeast's voice booms from speakers announcing the game. A giant scoreboard tracks your progress.  
**Puzzles:** Choose which door (color-coded by difficulty). Read the scoreboard for clues.  
**Educational:** Reading comprehension (signs, rules posted on walls).

### 2. 🏝️ Chandler's Island
**Concept:** A tropical island where Chandler has been "stranded" (again). He needs your help finding supplies. Palm trees, sand, a broken boat.  
**Puzzles:** Find coconuts, rope, and wood to fix the boat. Classic inventory puzzle.  
**Educational:** Sequencing (fix things in the right order), reading clues.

### 3. 🛒 The $100,000 Store
**Concept:** A recreated store where you have a budget and a timer. Shelves full of items with price tags. Grab what you need to solve the next challenge.  
**Puzzles:** Budget math — pick items that total exactly $100,000. Some items are traps.  
**Educational:** Addition, subtraction, comparison, budgeting.

### 4. 🔴 Squid Game Arena
**Concept:** A room with a giant doll on a pedestal, red and green lights, and a finish line. Follow the rules: move on GREEN, freeze on RED.  
**Puzzles:** Command-based timing puzzle. Type "move" only when the light is green.  
**Educational:** Following rules, reading instructions carefully, impulse control.

### 5. 🔒 The Vault
**Concept:** A massive safe room with a combination lock. Stacks of cash visible through a window. Notes and clues scattered on the floor hint at the combination.  
**Puzzles:** Gather clues from previous rooms to crack the code. Multi-step logic puzzle.  
**Educational:** Reading comprehension (clue notes), number patterns, deduction.

### 6. 🎯 The Obstacle Course
**Concept:** A gauntlet of challenges — foam pit, balance beam, climbing wall, slide — described with vivid sensory detail. Karl is at the start cheering you on.  
**Puzzles:** Navigate by choosing the right path. Some routes are faster but harder.  
**Educational:** Decision-making, risk assessment, spatial reasoning.

### 7. 🍫 The Feastables Factory
**Concept:** A Willy-Wonka-meets-MrBeast chocolate factory. Conveyor belts, mixing vats, chocolate rivers. Find the Golden Ticket hidden somewhere in the factory.  
**Puzzles:** Search containers, read ingredient lists for clues, combine items.  
**Educational:** Reading labels, following recipes (sequencing), vocabulary.

### 8. 🎮 Beast Games Control Room
**Concept:** Tareq is monitoring challenges on a wall of screens. Buttons, levers, and a microphone. You can influence what happens in other rooms from here.  
**Puzzles:** Read screen descriptions to figure out which button helps which room.  
**Educational:** Cause and effect, reading comprehension, logical reasoning.

### 9. 💰 The Million-Dollar Decision Room
**Concept:** Two podiums, two briefcases. Nolan stands across from you. One briefcase has $1,000,000. The other has nothing. MrBeast offers hints... for a price.  
**Puzzles:** Probability and deduction. Gather info, make your choice.  
**Educational:** Risk vs. reward, probability concepts, critical thinking.

### 10. 🌳 Team Trees Garden
**Concept:** An outdoor garden space representing MrBeast's charity work. Wilted plants need water. Trash needs picking up. A tree-planting station.  
**Puzzles:** Environmental cleanup — find and use the right tools. Giving-based solutions.  
**Educational:** Environmental awareness, cause and effect, teamwork/helping themes.

## Design Recommendations for the Team

1. **Pick 7 of the 10 room concepts** — the warehouse hub is a must; the rest can be chosen based on puzzle variety.
2. **Chandler as the comedic NPC** — he can appear in multiple rooms, always needing help (kids love this).
3. **Use the MrBeast video intro pattern** as the room-entry narration: "Welcome to [room]! Today, you have to [challenge]. The prize? [reward]!"
4. **Feastables as consumable items** — chocolate bars that give hints or energy.
5. **Money as the score system** — instead of points, track "prize money earned."
6. **Big reveals** — when the player solves a puzzle, make it feel HUGE. "YOU JUST WON $50,000!!!"
7. **Keep failure fun** — Chandler-style: "Oops! Even Chandler would've gotten that one. Try again!"
8. **Color-code everything** — red = danger, green = go, gold = treasure, blue = water/calm.
9. **The Golden Ticket** — a meta-puzzle spanning all rooms, like finding pieces of a Feastables wrapper.
10. **3rd-grade reading level** — short sentences, common words, exclamation energy.

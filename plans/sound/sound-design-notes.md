# Sound Design Notes**Author:** Comic Book Guy (Creative Director)  **Date:** 2026-07-28  **Status:** DESIGN COMPLETE  **Scope:** Game design perspective for sound effects implementation---## Game Design> "Sound in a text adventure? Heresy, you say? I remind you that Anchorhead and Sunless Sea proved atmosphere is not the enemy of imagination ΓÇö it is imagination's accelerant. The text remains primary. The sound is the ghost that haunts the text."### Design PhilosophyThis game starts at 2 AM in total darkness. The player navigates by FEEL, SMELL, and LISTEN before they ever see anything. Sound effects are the audio equivalent of `on_listen` ΓÇö they reinforce what the text already communicates. They are **atmospheric seasoning, not information delivery**.**Three Iron Laws of Sound in This Engine:**1. **Text is canonical.** Every sound event MUST have corresponding text output. A deaf player misses zero gameplay information. Sound adds emotional texture, never data.2. **Sound is opt-in.** The game works perfectly with sound disabled, muted, or on a platform that doesn't support audio. No puzzle, no clue, no mechanic depends on hearing a sound.3. **Sound follows state.** Sounds are triggered by FSM transitions and verb actions ΓÇö the same events that generate text. No orphan sounds that don't correspond to a game event.---### 1. Sound Priority TiersEvery sound category ranked by player impact. Accessibility Design#### 3.1 The Dual-Channel PrincipleEvery game event produces TWO outputs:1. **Text** (always present, always complete, always canonical)2. **Sound** (optional enhancement, adds emotional texture)The text is not a transcript of the sound. The text is the game. The sound is the atmosphere.```Player types: OPEN DOORGame outputs:  TEXT:  "The heavy oak door groans open on iron hinges."  SOUND: [door-creak-heavy.ogg plays]Player types: OPEN DOOR (with sound muted)Game outputs:  TEXT:  "The heavy oak door groans open on iron hinges."  SOUND: (nothing ΓÇö game is identical)```#### 3.2 What Sound Must NEVER Do- **Never convey unique gameplay information.** If a sound plays that tells you "a rat is in the next room," the text MUST also say something equivalent. The `on_listen` descriptions already handle this ΓÇö they're the text channel.- **Never be required for a puzzle.** No "listen for the right number of clicks" puzzles. If a puzzle involves sound, the text describes the sound ("You hear three clicks, then a grinding sound").- **Never replace `on_listen` text.** The `on_listen` field continues to serve as the canonical auditory description. Sound files are an *additional* parallel channel.- **Never block the game loop.** On web (Web Audio API), sounds are async. On terminal, if a platform doesn't support sound, it's a no-op. No freezing, no waiting.#### 3.3 Player Controls| Control | Default | Notes ||---------|---------|-------|| Master volume | 80% | Single slider, 0ΓÇô100% || Sound effects toggle | ON | On/off for all SFX || Ambient toggle | ON | Separate from SFX ΓÇö some players want action sounds but not loops || Creature sound toggle | ON | Some players find creature sounds anxiety-inducing ΓÇö respect that || Text-only mode | OFF | When ON, suppresses all audio (equivalent to muting, but explicit) |**No separate volume sliders for SFX vs. ambient vs. creatures in MVP.** A single master volume + three toggles is sufficient. Full mixer UI is Phase 2+ if needed.#### 3.4 Screen Reader CompatibilitySound files MUST NOT interfere with screen reader output. The sound layer:- Does not generate any text of its own- Does not consume keyboard input- Does not modify the DOM in ways that trigger aria-live announcements- Plays at a volume that doesn't mask TTS output (duck to 50% if TTS detected ΓÇö future consideration)---### 4. This prevents audio mud.---### 5. Combat Sound Design#### 5.1 Combat Sound LayeringCombat involves multiple simultaneous events: creature vocalizations, weapon impacts, player reactions, and narration text. The challenge is keeping it legible ΓÇö both aurally and textually.**Combat Sound Budget: 3 simultaneous sounds maximum.**Any more and the audio becomes noise. The three slots:| Slot | Purpose | Example ||------|---------|---------|| **Slot 1: Ambient** | Room ambient continues at reduced volume (ducked 50%) | `amb-cellar-drip.ogg` at 50% || **Slot 2: Creature** | The creature's current-state vocalization | `wolf-snarl.ogg` || **Slot 3: Impact** | The hit/miss/block sound for the current exchange | `impact-bite.ogg` |**Why no Slot 4?** Because the text is doing heavy lifting. The combat narration ("The wolf lunges, jaws snapping. Its teeth find your forearm ΓÇö a sharp, tearing pain.") provides all the detail. The sound sells the *moment*; the text sells the *story*.#### 5.2 Combat Sound Events| Event | Sound | Text Parallel | Priority ||-------|-------|--------------|----------|| **Creature enters aggressive state** | Creature vocalization (e.g., `wolf-snarl.ogg`) | "The wolf's hackles rise. Sound should reflect damage type, not weapon identity:| Damage Type | Sound | Description ||-------------|-------|-------------|| **Pierce** (bite, stab, needle) | `impact-pierce.ogg` | Sharp, quick, wet || **Slash** (claw, dagger, glass shard) | `impact-slash.ogg` | Tearing, swift || **Blunt** (club, crowbar, falling rock) | `impact-blunt.ogg` | Heavy, dull thud || **Crush** (bear trap, ceiling collapse) | `impact-crush.ogg` | Grinding, heavy, sustained |**4 impact sounds cover all combat.** Weapon-specific sounds are Phase 2 polish.#### 5.4 Combat PacingCombat in this game uses a 6-phase exchange FSM (per the combat plan). Sound must not rush ahead of text:1. **Phase 1 (Engagement):** Creature vocalization plays. Text describes the creature's posture.2. **Phase 2ΓÇô4 (Exchange):** Impact sound plays on resolution. Text narrates the blow.3. **Phase 5 (Result):** Injury sound plays if applicable. Text describes the wound.4. **Phase 6 (Reset/Flee):** Creature state-change sound plays. Text describes what happens next.**Each phase gets at most ONE event sound.** The engine should queue sounds and play them in sequence with the text, not dump everything at once.---### 6.

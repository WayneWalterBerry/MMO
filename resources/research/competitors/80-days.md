## What It Is

80 Days casts the player as Passepartout, valet to Phileas Fogg, planning routes across 150+ cities in a steampunk alternate-history world. Players balance **time management, finances, Fogg's health**, and narrative choices while discovering secrets, trading items, and uncovering stories. The game features **real-time multiplayer positioning** (see other players' journeys on the globe) and Meg Jayanth's acclaimed writing (750,000+ words).

Named **TIME Magazine's Game of the Year 2014**. Four BAFTA nominations.

## What Players LOVE

- **Extraordinary writing** — 750K+ words of Meg Jayanth's prose; every route feels unique
- **Incredible replay value** — millions of possible journeys; no two playthroughs alike
- **Steampunk world** — rich alternate history with diverse, decolonized perspectives
- **Time pressure** — the 80-day deadline creates genuine tension without being punishing
- **Route planning strategy** — resource management adds depth beyond just reading
- **See other players** — asynchronous multiplayer shows real-time player positions globally
- **Graphic novel aesthetic** — beautiful art style that complements the text
- **Can always "win"** — but the goal is to see more of the world, not just finish fast

## What Players HATE

- **Route discovery complexity** — some optimal routes are hard to find
- **No travel advice log** — hard to track important information between cities
- **Can feel overwhelming** — so many possible routes that choice paralysis sets in
- **Occasionally unfair RNG** — some events feel random rather than consequential
- **Text-heavy** — players expecting action will be disappointed

## How It Compares to Our Project

### What 80 Days does that we don't
- **Asynchronous multiplayer visualization** — seeing other players on the globe creates community without direct interaction (elegant)
- **Massive authored content** — 750K words of professional prose
- **Visual presentation** — graphic novel art elevates the text experience
- **Time pressure mechanic** — 80-day deadline creates compelling pacing
- **BAFTA-nominated production** — studio-level polish
- **Decolonized worldbuilding** — diverse perspectives are a key differentiator

### What we do that 80 Days doesn't
- **Parser-based interaction** — free-form text input vs. presented choices
- **Real-time multiplayer** — actual shared world vs. asynchronous visualization
- **Self-modifying universe** — mutable game state vs. fixed narrative branches
- **Object interaction** — physical world with FSM-driven objects
- **Tactile exploration** — room-by-room discovery vs. city-to-city travel
- **Procedural generation** — infinite worlds vs. 150 hand-crafted cities

### Strategic Insight

80 Days is the **creative benchmark** for mobile interactive fiction. Its key innovation is **asynchronous social multiplayer** (seeing other players on the globe) which creates a sense of community and competition without requiring real-time interaction. This is directly relevant to our multiverse — we could show other players' universe states or journeys without requiring synchronous play.

**Key lessons:**
1. **Asynchronous multiplayer is powerful** — low cost, high emotional impact
2. **Time pressure creates pacing** — a ticking clock makes every choice matter
3. **Enormous content volume** (750K words) drives replay — our procedural generation must match this depth
4. **The globe/map is the killer feature** — spatial visualization transforms the experience

## What It Is

Episode is the **dominant player** in the mobile interactive story space by sheer scale — 170M+ lifetime downloads, 150,000+ stories, and $1M+/month revenue. It features animated 3D characters, branching stories across romance/drama/adventure genres, and a massive **user-generated content ecosystem** where players can create their own stories.

Episode represents the **mass-market extreme** of interactive fiction — prioritizing visual presentation, social features, and freemium monetization over narrative depth or mechanical complexity.

## What Players LOVE

- **Massive story library** — 150,000+ stories across genres
- **Avatar customization** — detailed character creation
- **User-generated content** — players can write and publish their own stories
- **Social features** — book clubs, reading challenges, community
- **Regular updates** — constant new content across multiple languages (6+)
- **Branching choices** — decisions affect story outcomes
- **Visual presentation** — animated characters make stories feel like TV shows

## What Players HATE

- **Premium currency** — best choices often locked behind gems/passes
- **Pay-to-play feel** — "free" but optimal experience costs real money
- **Romance-heavy** — genre diversity is limited; most popular stories are romance
- **"Choice" is an illusion** — many stories funnel to the same outcome regardless
- **Aggressive monetization** — constant upselling and gem prompts
- **Quality variance** — user-generated stories range from excellent to terrible
- **Wait timers** — passes regenerate slowly; must pay or wait to continue

## How It Compares to Our Project

### What Episode does that we don't
- **170M downloads** — defines the mass market for mobile interactive fiction
- **Visual presentation** — 3D animated characters vs. our pure text
- **Content ecosystem** — 150,000 user-generated stories; self-sustaining content flywheel
- **Massive revenue** — proves interactive fiction can be a real business on mobile
- **Social features** — book clubs, challenges, community engagement

### What we do that Episode doesn't
- **Parser-based exploration** — we offer real text adventure; Episode is tap-to-choose
- **No predatory monetization** — we don't gate choices behind premium currency
- **Mechanical depth** — FSM objects, inventory, spatial world; Episode has no game mechanics
- **Self-modifying world** — Lua-driven dynamic state
- **Multiplayer/MMO** — shared universe (Episode is single-player stories)
- **Respect for player intelligence** — we don't dumb down choices for monetization

### Strategic Insight

Episode is **not our direct competitor** — it targets a completely different audience (teens, romance readers, casual mobile gamers). However, it provides critical market intelligence:

1. **The interactive fiction mobile market is HUGE** — 170M downloads, $1M/month revenue
2. **User-generated content is a growth engine** — if we ever open world-building to players, this is the model
3. **Visual presentation scales audience** — pure text will always be niche compared to visual novels
4. **Predatory monetization works financially** but creates player resentment — we should avoid this
5. **Our audience is NOT this audience** — we target IF enthusiasts, gamers, and people who read; Episode targets TikTok-generation visual consumers

**We don't need to beat Episode. We need to be the best text adventure for people who think Episode is shallow.**

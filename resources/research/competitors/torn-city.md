## What It Is

Torn City is a persistent, browser-based text MMO set in a modern-day criminal underworld. Players commit crimes, join factions, fight other players, manage businesses, trade on markets, and progress through a complex web of activities — all via text and menus. Often described as "GTA in text form," it's one of the longest-running text-based MMOs, active since 2004.

The mobile app is essentially a **wrapper for the web version**, which is both its strength (full feature access) and weakness (not natively designed for mobile).

## What Players LOVE

- **Incredible depth** — crimes, factions, businesses, stocks, property, education, social systems
- **Player-driven economy** — real trading, investing, and market manipulation
- **Faction warfare** — organized PvP with politics, diplomacy, and betrayal
- **20+ year legacy** — massive accumulated content and established community
- **"GTA in text"** — appeals to players who want crime-sim without graphics
- **Free to play** — no paywall for core experience
- **Active community** — forums, Discord, player-run newspapers

## What Players HATE

- **Clunky mobile UI** — the app is a browser wrapper; not natively designed for mobile
- **Extremely slow progression** — requires daily login for months/years to see real progress
- **Overwhelming for newcomers** — massive system with poor onboarding
- **Pay-to-win concerns** — subscribers and big spenders have significant advantages
- **Time commitment** — success requires constant attention; punishes casual play
- **Dated interface** — looks and feels like a 2004 web game (because it is)
- **iOS app quality** — 3.0 stars on iOS reflects poor mobile experience

## How It Compares to Our Project

### What Torn City does that we don't
- **Persistent MMO with 1M+ players** — proven that text MMO can attract massive audience
- **Player economy** — deep market, trading, and business systems
- **Faction warfare** — organized multiplayer PvP
- **20 years of accumulated content and systems**

### What we do that Torn City doesn't
- **Parser-based exploration** — we offer real text adventure; Torn is menu-clicking
- **On-device play** — no server dependency; Torn requires constant internet
- **Self-modifying world** — our universe mutates; Torn's rules are fixed
- **Modern UX** — our PWA will be designed mobile-first; Torn is a browser wrapper
- **Narrative depth** — we're building story; Torn is systems-focused with minimal narrative
- **No pay-to-win** — our architecture doesn't support paid advantages

### Strategic Insight

Torn City proves **text-based MMO can reach 1 million+ downloads** and sustain a 20-year community. But it also shows the pitfalls:

1. **Browser-wrapper apps rate poorly** (3.0 on iOS) — our PWA must feel native
2. **Slow progression kills retention** — our game needs satisfying short-session loops
3. **Pay-to-win destroys trust** — our zero-cost-per-player model is an advantage
4. **Onboarding is critical** — Torn overwhelms newcomers; our "start in darkness" gradually reveals complexity

**Market validation:** 1M+ downloads for a 20-year-old text MMO proves the audience exists and is larger than the IF community assumes.

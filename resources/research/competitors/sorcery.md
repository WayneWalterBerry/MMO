## What It Is

Sorcery! is a four-part interactive gamebook adventure based on Steve Jackson's classic *Sorcery!* Fighting Fantasy books. Players navigate a fantasy world via a **hand-drawn map**, making choices, casting spells (from a system of 48 spells), and engaging in a unique **bluff-based combat system**. The game uses inkle's proprietary **ink** scripting language with rich visual presentation.

This is arguably the **gold standard for mobile interactive fiction** — it combines text narrative with visual map exploration, creating an experience that feels like a tabletop RPG.

## What Players LOVE

- **Hand-drawn map navigation** — drag your character across a gorgeous illustrated map
- **48 spell system** — memorize three-letter codes to cast spells with surprising story effects
- **Bluff-based combat** — unique strategic combat that replaces dice rolling
- **Massive branching** — thousands of possible paths; genuine replay value
- **Faithful yet expanded adaptation** — respects the source material while adding depth
- **Beautiful art** — John Blanche illustrations, Eddie Sharam character art
- **Atmospheric soundtrack** — complements the fantasy world
- **Four interconnected games** — choices carry across all four parts

## What Players HATE

- **Part 1 is short** — some feel the first entry is too brief
- **Text-heavy** — players wanting action RPG combat may find it slow
- **Complex for newcomers** — the spell system and combat can be confusing initially
- **$20 for the full series** — premium pricing across 4 separate purchases
- **Occasional UI issues** — map navigation can be finicky on small screens

## How It Compares to Our Project

### What Sorcery! does that we don't
- **Visual map** — spatial navigation via illustrated map; we're pure text
- **Published IP** — Steve Jackson's brand brings an existing audience
- **Art and music** — significant production value investment
- **Rewind mechanic** — players can undo choices freely; reduces frustration
- **4-part saga** — long-form narrative across connected episodes
- **Polished production** — inkle is a professional studio with resources

### What we do that Sorcery! doesn't
- **Free-form parser input** — Sorcery! is choice-based; we allow typed exploration
- **Multiplayer/MMO** — shared world vs. solo adventure
- **Self-modifying universe** — our Lua world mutates; Sorcery! is fixed authored content
- **Procedural generation** — infinite worlds vs. hand-crafted levels
- **PWA deployment** — browser-based, no app store
- **Object interaction depth** — FSM-driven objects with complex states

### Strategic Insight

Sorcery! demonstrates that **adding visual elements (maps, art) dramatically expands audience** compared to pure text IF. The hand-drawn map solves spatial navigation without requiring a parser — players see where they are and tap to move.

**Key lessons:**
1. **Rewind/undo is essential** — Sorcery!'s undo mechanic is beloved; our event-sourced architecture supports this natively
2. **Maps matter** — even a simple text-rendered map of our rooms could significantly improve navigation
3. **Spell systems create mastery** — the 48-spell memorization mechanic creates player investment; our self-modifying world could enable similar "learn the system" depth
4. **Premium pricing works for quality** — $5/part × 4 = $20 total, and players pay happily for the quality

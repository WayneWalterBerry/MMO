## NEW BUGS

### BUG-033 (MEDIUM): GOAP Relight Fails When Spent Match in Hand

**Severity:** MEDIUM — Blocks common play pattern (extinguish → relight cycle)  
**Reproducibility:** 100%

**Steps to reproduce:**
1. `light candle` (GOAP succeeds, match auto-spends, stays in left hand)
2. `blow candle`
3. `light candle`

**Expected:** GOAP should auto-drop the spent match, take a fresh match from matchbox, strike it, and relight the candle.

**Actual:**
```
You'll need to prepare first...

Your hands are full. Drop something first.
The match is spent. It cannot be relit.

You have nothing to relight it with.
```

**Root cause:** The GOAP planner doesn't account for spent matches occupying hand slots. When it checks for a free hand to take a new match, it finds hands full (spent match in one hand). It also tries to strike the spent match instead of acquiring a fresh one. The planner needs to either:
- Auto-drop spent matches as part of the plan
- Skip spent matches when checking for fire_source capability
- Include "drop spent items" as a prerequisite step

**Workaround:** Player must manually `drop match` before `light candle` after each extinguish cycle.

---

### BUG-034 (MINOR): "put out candle" Parsed as PUT Verb

**Severity:** MINOR — Common phrasing not recognized  
**Reproducibility:** 100%

**Steps to reproduce:**
1. Light candle
2. `put out candle`

**Expected:** Candle should be extinguished (same as `blow candle` or `extinguish candle`).

**Actual:**
```
Put what where? (Try: put <item> in/on <target>)
```

**Root cause:** Parser matches "put" as the PUT verb before recognizing "put out" as the phrasal verb for extinguish. The `blow` and `extinguish` synonyms work, but "put out" is treated as a two-word phrase where "put" captures the verb.

**Fix:** Add "put out" as a recognized phrase that routes to the extinguish handler, or add special-case handling in the parser for phrasal verbs.

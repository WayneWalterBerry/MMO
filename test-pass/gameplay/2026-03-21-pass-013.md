## Bug Reports

### BUG-048: Iron key inaccessible inside crate after prying it open 🔴 CRITICAL

**Steps to reproduce:**
```
> get crowbar
You take an iron crowbar.
> open crate
You jam the crowbar under the lid and heave. Nails shriek as they pull free,
and the lid comes away in a shower of splinters and rust. Inside: a heavy sack
nestled in straw packing.
> look in crate
You can't see in a pried-open crate right now.
> get iron key
You don't see that here.
```

**Expected:** After prying the crate open, the iron key inside `large-crate.inside` should become accessible — visible via `look in crate` and gettable with `get iron key`.

**Actual:** The crate opens (great animation text!) but the `.inside` surface contents are never exposed. The iron key is permanently trapped inside, making it impossible to unlock the north door to deep-cellar.

**Impact:** 🔴 BLOCKS THE ENTIRE LEVEL 1 CRITICAL PATH. Cannot progress from storage-cellar to deep-cellar to hallway. The crate puzzle is the gatekeeper for all downstream rooms.

**Root cause (probable):** The `open` mutation on large-crate transitions to "pried-open" state but doesn't mark the `.inside` surface as accessible/visible. The containment system doesn't expose hidden surface contents when the container state changes.

### BUG-049: Parser doesn't understand "pry X with Y" or "open X with Y" 🟡 MAJOR

**Steps to reproduce:**
```
> pry crate with crowbar
I don't understand that.

> open crate with crowbar
You don't see that here.
```

**Expected:** Both phrasings should work to open the crate using the crowbar.

**Actual:** "pry" is not a recognized verb. "open X with Y" fails because the parser can't handle the "with" preposition (treats "crowbar" as the target, not the tool). The bare `open crate` works because the engine auto-detects the crowbar in hand, but players will naturally try the explicit phrasings first.

**Workaround:** `open crate` (bare) works if crowbar is in hand.

**Impact:** Player frustration. The crate description says "nailed fast" — the natural impulse is to name the tool explicitly.

**Impact:** Major immersion/aesthetic issue. The hallway and crypt are the worst affected rooms since they have the most duplicate type instances.

### BUG-051: Courtyard moonlight not recognized by light system 🟠 MEDIUM

**Steps to reproduce:**
```
> lua src/main.lua --no-ui --room courtyard
> look
The Inner Courtyard
It is too dark to see. You need a light source.
```

**Expected:** The courtyard is outdoors with moonlight (room `light_level = 1`). The description says "a half-moon casting silver light over everything." The player should be able to see — at least dimly.

**Actual:** The engine's `get_light_level()` only checks for `casts_light` on objects or daylight/curtain logic. It ignores the room's own `light_level` field entirely. The courtyard is treated as pitch black even though it's an outdoor moonlit space.

**Impact:** The courtyard is only usable with a carried light source. Player who reaches it without a candle gets "too dark" in an outdoor space described as moonlit — a contradiction.

### BUG-052: Sarcophagus ambiguity in crypt — 5 identical names, no way to target specific ones 🟠 MEDIUM

**Steps to reproduce:**
```
> feel (in crypt)
  a stone sarcophagus
  a stone sarcophagus
  a stone sarcophagus
  a stone sarcophagus
  a stone sarcophagus
```

**Expected:** Some way to distinguish or target individual sarcophagi — ordinals ("first sarcophagus"), positional names ("south sarcophagus"), or numbering.

**Actual:** All 5 are identical "a stone sarcophagus" with the same type_id. Player can only interact with whichever one the engine picks first. Can't target specific ones (sarcophagus-2 holds the bronze ring, -3 has the silver dagger, etc.).

**Impact:** Player can't explore the crypt puzzle properly. Important items hidden inside specific sarcophagi may be unreachable or require random luck.

## 1. GOAP Natural Language Commands (THE BIG TEST) 🎉

### 1a. Cold-Start "light candle" — THE SHOWSTOPPER

**Setup:** Fresh game. Absolute darkness. Nothing opened, nothing in hand.

| # | Input | Output | Status |
|---|-------|--------|--------|
| 1 | `light candle` | "You'll need to prepare first..." → auto-chains: open drawer → open matchbox → take match → strike match → light candle | ✅ 🎉 |

**GOAP auto-chain sequence (from cold start):**
```
> light candle
You'll need to prepare first...
You pull the small drawer open. It slides out with a soft wooden scrape.
You slide the matchbox tray open with your thumb. Inside, a clutch of wooden matches rests snugly in a row.
You take a wooden match from an open matchbox.
You drag the match head across the striker strip. It sputters once, twice -- then catches...
The wick catches the flame and curls to life, throwing a warm amber glow across the room.
```

**Verdict:** 🎉 **OUTSTANDING.** One command from darkness to lit room. Five prerequisite actions auto-chained flawlessly. The "You'll need to prepare first..." intro text is perfect player feedback. Candle lit on nightstand (efficient — didn't pick up matchbox or candle unnecessarily). This is a MAJOR quality-of-life improvement.

### 1b. GOAP Phrasing Variants

| # | Input | Cold Start? | Result | Status |
|---|-------|-------------|--------|--------|
| 1 | `light candle` | Yes | Full 5-step auto-chain | ✅ |
| 2 | `light the candle` | Yes | Full 5-step auto-chain (article stripped) | ✅ |
| 3 | `light candle with match` | Yes | Full 5-step auto-chain | ✅ |
| 4 | `ignite candle` | Yes | Full 5-step auto-chain (synonym recognized) | ✅ |
| 5 | `burn candle` | Yes | "You have no flame to burn anything with." (no GOAP) | ⚠️ |
| 6 | `get match from matchbox and light candle` | Yes | First half fails ("You don't see matchbox here"), second half GOAP chains | ⚠️ |
| 7 | `take a match and strike it` | Yes | Both halves fail — "You don't see that here." / "You don't see anything to strike against." | ❌ |
| 8 | `strike a match` | Yes | "You don't see anything to strike against." (no GOAP) | ❌ |
| 9 | `illuminate the room` | Yes | "I don't understand that." | ❌ |
| 10 | `make light` | Yes | "I don't understand that." | ❌ |
| 11 | `use match on candle` | Yes | "I don't understand that." | ❌ |

**Analysis:**
- **Core GOAP goal words work:** `light`, `ignite` + candle triggers full backward chain. ✅
- **Article handling works:** "the candle" → candle. ✅
- **Instrument clause works:** "with match" recognized. ✅
- **GOAP only triggers for registered goal verbs.** `burn`, `strike`, `use X on Y` do NOT trigger GOAP chaining — they hit the regular parser which checks immediate preconditions and fails.
- **BUG-031 (MINOR):** Compound "and" commands split literally, first half can fail while second half GOAP-succeeds, showing confusing mixed output ("You don't see matchbox here" followed by GOAP success).
- **BUG-032 (MINOR):** "burn candle" recognizes the verb and reports "no flame" but doesn't trigger GOAP to GET a flame. Inconsistent with "light candle" which does chain. Player would expect "burn" to behave like "light."

## 7. New Bugs

### BUG-031 (MINOR): Compound "and" commands show confusing mixed output with GOAP

**Severity:** Minor  
**Repro:**
1. Fresh game start (darkness)
2. Type: `get match from matchbox and light candle`
3. **Expected:** Either GOAP chains everything, or fails gracefully
4. **Actual:** First half fails with "You don't see matchbox here." Then GOAP kicks in for "light candle" and succeeds. Player sees an error message followed by success — confusing.

### BUG-032 (MINOR): "burn candle" doesn't trigger GOAP but "light candle" does

**Severity:** Minor  
**Repro:**
1. Fresh game start (darkness)
2. Type: `burn candle`
3. **Expected:** GOAP chains to get a flame, then burn/light candle (same as `light candle`)
4. **Actual:** "You have no flame to burn anything with." — verb is recognized, precondition checked, but no GOAP backward-chaining. `light candle` and `ignite candle` DO trigger GOAP. "burn" should be a synonym or also registered as a GOAP goal.

## Real-World Logic

**Premise:** The Molotov cocktail — a glass bottle filled with flammable liquid with a cloth wick — is the most famous improvised incendiary device in history. First used in the Spanish Civil War, named during the Winter War. But the concept is older: oil-soaked rags thrown into enemy fortifications date back millennia. A glass bottle of oil + a rag + fire = a thrown fire weapon.

**Why it's satisfying:** A wooden barricade blocks the way. It's too solid to break, too heavy to move. But it's *wood*. It *burns*. The player has an empty wine bottle, an oil flask, and a rag. The solution assembles itself in the player's mind: fill bottle with oil, stuff rag in as a wick, light the wick, throw the bottle at the barricade. CRASH. WHOOSH. Path clear.

**What makes it real:** This is the escalation of the improvised torch (Puzzle 021). Same components (rag, oil, fire), but weaponized. The player who learned "rag + oil = portable fire" now learns "rag + oil + bottle = throwable fire." Knowledge compounds.

## Overview

A passage is blocked by a thick wooden barricade — nailed boards, dry timber, or dense spider webbing (if a natural variant). The barricade is too heavy to move, too strong to break with available tools, but visibly made of flammable material. The player must construct an incendiary device and destroy the barrier.

This puzzle escalates the fire-crafting system: from lighting a candle (Puzzle 001) to making a torch (Puzzle 021) to constructing a fire-bomb (this puzzle). Each step builds on the previous, teaching increasingly complex fire manipulation.

## Solution Path

### Primary Solution (Oil Bomb — Thrown)
1. Player has empty wine-bottle (or drinks/pours wine out first)
2. `POUR oil INTO bottle` — fill empty bottle with oil from oil-flask
3. `STUFF rag INTO bottle` — push rag into neck, leaving a tail hanging out
4. `LIGHT rag WITH match` — ignite the protruding wick
5. `THROW bottle AT barricade` — bottle shatters on impact, oil sprays, fire erupts
6. **Result:** Barricade burns. After several turns, passage is clear.
7. ⚠️ **Warning:** Player must STAND BACK before throwing — fire spreads, and splash damage causes burn injury if too close.

### Alternative Solution A (Direct Torch Burn)
1. Player has lit torch (existing or improvised from Puzzle 021)
2. `HOLD torch AGAINST barricade` — sets wood on fire directly
3. **Result:** Slower burn — takes 5–8 turns. Player must wait. Risk of smoke inhalation in enclosed space.
4. **Trade-off:** Simpler but slower and smokier.

### Alternative Solution B (Pour Oil and Light)
1. `POUR oil ON barricade` — douse the wood in oil
2. `LIGHT barricade WITH match` — direct ignition
3. **Result:** Burns hot and fast — but uncontrolled. Fire spreads to nearby objects.
4. **Trade-off:** Fast but dangerous to surroundings.

### Alternative Solution C (Find Alternate Path)
1. If barricade isn't the only route — find a hidden passage, vent, or alternate room
2. Puzzle 022 (Smoke Draft Reveal) technique might find a bypass
3. **Result:** No fire needed. Barricade remains.

## Failure Modes

| Failure | Consequence | Recovery |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Stand too close when throwing | Splash-back burn injury (5 damage — Effects Pipeline) | Treat burn, wait for barricade fire to subside |
| Light bomb but don't throw fast enough | Bomb detonates in hand — severe burn (10 damage) | Drop immediately; treat injury; try again with new bomb |
| Fire spreads to unintended objects | Nearby crate, cloth, or wooden furniture catches fire | Stamp out or let burn — collateral damage |
| Smoke fills enclosed passage | Coughing, visibility reduced, slow suffocation | Move away; wait for smoke to clear; find ventilation |
| Use all oil for bomb — lantern can't be refueled | No more oil for light sources | Candles/matches still work; bomb was a resource trade-off |
| Throw and miss | Bottle shatters on floor — fire spreads on floor, not barricade | Dangerous, but fire may still reach barricade. Escape the area. |

## Design Rationale

**Why fire-bomb?** It's the ultimate expression of the fire crafting system. The game teaches fire in stages: light a candle (safe), make a torch (useful), build a bomb (dangerous). Each step raises the stakes and the reward. The fire-bomb is the player's graduation from fire-student to fire-master.

**Why Level 4?** Multi-step crafting (4 actions to assemble + throw). Spatial awareness required (stand back). Environmental consequences (fire spread, smoke). Resource consumption is permanent. Self-injury on misuse is significant. This is advanced fire manipulation.

**Why the barricade?** It provides a clear problem (path blocked) with a clear physical solution (burn it). The barricade is obviously wood, obviously dry, obviously flammable. The challenge is in the MAKING of the fire-bomb, not in the IDENTIFICATION of the problem.

## GOAP Analysis

GOAP cannot resolve fire-bomb construction. The planner has no recipe for "improvised incendiary." GOAP might resolve "destroy barricade" → need fire → need fire source → light torch → apply to barricade (Alternative A), but the thrown fire-bomb (Primary) is pure player crafting.

**GOAP-resolved:** Torch-based direct burning (Alternative A).  
**Manual:** Fire-bomb assembly and tactical deployment.

## Notes & Edge Cases

- **Indoor vs. outdoor:** Indoor fire-bomb in enclosed space = smoke danger. Outdoor = wind might affect fire direction.
- **Web variant:** Spider webs burn even faster than wood. A web-blocked passage ignites instantly — dramatic and satisfying.
- **Collateral damage:** Fire can spread to objects the player hasn't picked up yet. A crate near the barricade might burn, destroying its contents.
- **Water as extinguisher:** If fire spreads unintentionally, water (rain-barrel, well-bucket) can extinguish it.
- **One-time use:** The fire-bomb is consumed on use. Oil flask may still have oil left (depends on how much was poured).
- **No softlock:** Direct torch burning (Alternative A) always works. Fire-bomb is the dramatic method, not the required one.

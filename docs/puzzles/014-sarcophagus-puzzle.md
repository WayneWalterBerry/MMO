## Overview

The Crypt is the deepest, oldest, and most sacred space in Level 1. Five stone sarcophagi line the walls, their lids carved with effigies of robed figures — the manor's original inhabitants. Each sarcophagus contains different contents: some hold only bones and dust, one holds the tome (a lore book that reveals the manor's dark history), and others hold burial goods (silver dagger, jewelry, coins). The player must open the heavy stone lids to discover what lies within.

The "puzzle" is less about mechanical challenge and more about exploration, deduction, and reward. The heavy lids test the player's understanding of physical manipulation (PUSH, LIFT, SLIDE — not just OPEN). The effigies on the lids provide clues about which sarcophagus might contain the most significant treasure — the player who examines the carvings carefully can prioritize. But all sarcophagi are openable, so there's no failure state.

This puzzle embodies Frink's research on discovery and exploration (§7.3, Idea 9: "The Chained Lockbox"): the crypt is the end of a long puzzle chain (move rug → find key → unlock cellar → find iron key → unlock deep cellar → perform ritual → unlock crypt). Reaching it IS the reward. The contents are the bonus.

The design philosophy is borrowed from Return of the Obra Dinn (Frink §2.3 [15][16]): pure observation and deduction, no inventory puzzles. The player examines effigies, reads inscriptions, and pieces together a story from physical evidence. The tome is the capstone — a narrative payoff for the most thorough explorers.

## Solution Path

### Primary Solution: Open Sarcophagi, Find Treasures

1. **Enter the Crypt** — Player arrives through the stone archway from the Deep Cellar. The room is small, cold, and solemn. Five sarcophagi line the walls. Candle stubs sit in wall niches, unlit.

2. **Light the crypt** — Player can light the candle stubs using their fire source for additional illumination and atmosphere. Optional but enriches the experience. Message: "You touch your flame to the old candle. It catches, sputtering at first, then steadying. A warm glow fills the niche, casting long shadows across the stone faces of the dead."

3. **EXAMINE sarcophagi** — Each sarcophagus has a distinct carved effigy on its lid:
   - **Sarcophagus A:** A robed figure holding a book. Inscription: "BROTHER ALDRIC — Keeper of Wisdom"
   - **Sarcophagus B:** A robed figure holding a sword. Inscription: "SISTER ELARA — Protector of the Faithful"
   - **Sarcophagus C:** A robed figure with hands folded in prayer. Inscription: "BROTHER MARCUS — First Among the Devoted"
   - **Sarcophagus D:** A robed figure holding a key. Inscription: "SISTER THEA — Guardian of Secrets"
   - **Sarcophagus E:** A robed figure with no symbol, face obscured. Inscription: worn smooth, illegible.

4. **PUSH lid** or **LIFT lid** or **SLIDE lid** on any sarcophagus — The lids are heavy stone. The player must exert force.
   - Message: "You brace your feet and push against the stone lid. It grinds slowly, scraping across the sarcophagus rim with a sound like grinding teeth. Dust cascades from the edges. The lid slides aside, revealing the interior."
   - Note: This is a strength action, not a tool action. No crowbar needed — the lids are heavy but not locked. The verb choice (PUSH vs. LIFT) doesn't matter mechanically but provides different flavor text.

5. **Contents of each sarcophagus:**

| Sarcophagus | Effigy Clue | Contents | Significance |
|-------------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| A (Aldric, book) | "Keeper of Wisdom" | **Tome** — the critical lore book | Reveals manor's founding, its purpose, the order that built it, and hints at Level 2+ mysteries |
| B (Elara, sword) | "Protector of the Faithful" | **Silver dagger** + bones wrapped in rusted chainmail | Weapon/tool for future levels; the chainmail tells a story of martial duty |
| C (Marcus, prayer) | "First Among the Devoted" | Bones only, folded hands, faded vestments | Nothing material — but EXAMINE reveals a prayer inscribed inside the lid |
| D (Thea, key) | "Guardian of Secrets" | **Burial jewelry** (silver ring, pendant) + small leather pouch with **coins** | Treasure with potential trade/barter value in future levels |
| E (obscured face) | Illegible inscription | **Empty** — the sarcophagus has been opened before. Scratch marks inside. Something was here. Something was *taken*. | Mystery hook: who was buried here? What was taken? This is a Level 2+ narrative seed. |

6. **READ tome** (from Sarcophagus A) — The tome reveals:
   - The manor was built by a religious order called "The Keepers of the Vigil"
   - They watched over something beneath the manor — something ancient and dangerous
   - The order disappeared generations ago under mysterious circumstances
   - The crypt holds the order's founders, who swore to guard the secret "even in death"
   - A warning: "What sleeps below must never wake"
   - (Exact text: TBD by Wayne — this is the major lore revelation of Level 1)

7. **EXAMINE wall inscriptions** — Carved text on the crypt walls provides dates, blessings, and names that corroborate the tome's history. One inscription reads: "We who keep the vigil shall not falter. Let no flame die. Let no door open. Let the sleepers rest."

### Deduction Layer (Optional Depth)

A player who examines ALL effigies before opening any sarcophagi can make informed choices:
- "Keeper of Wisdom" + book symbol → likely contains the lore text
- "Protector of the Faithful" + sword → likely contains a weapon
- "Guardian of Secrets" + key → likely contains hidden valuables
- Folded hands, prayer → likely contains nothing material
- Obscured face, illegible → mysterious — something unusual

This is Obra Dinn-style deduction (Frink §2.3): the player observes visible evidence (effigies, inscriptions) and predicts hidden content. The deduction isn't required (all lids are openable), but it rewards the observant player with the satisfaction of confirmed predictions.

## What the Player Learns

1. **Heavy object manipulation** — Stone lids require PUSH, LIFT, or SLIDE — not just OPEN. The physical weight of objects matters. This introduces strength-based interaction for future levels where heavy objects may be obstacles (Per Frink §4.1: real-world problem-solving).
2. **Environmental observation as deduction** — Effigies and inscriptions are clues, not just decoration. The player who examines before acting makes better choices. Per Frink's Obra Dinn analysis (§2.3 [15]): "Strip detective work to observation and logic."
3. **Lore rewards exploration** — The tome is the deepest lore object in Level 1, obtainable only by: moving the rug → finding the trap door → descending → finding two keys → performing a ritual → opening a sarcophagus. This is a 10+ step chain. The reward must match the effort.
4. **Not everything holds treasure** — Sarcophagus C (bones only) and E (empty) teach that exploration doesn't always yield tangible rewards — but it always yields information. The empty sarcophagus is the most intriguing find: who was here? What was taken?
5. **Mystery hooks create forward momentum** — The empty sarcophagus and the tome's warnings ("what sleeps below must never wake") create narrative tension that propels the player into Level 2. Per Emily Short (Frink §1.3 [7]): "Puzzles as narrative rewards."

## Failure Modes & Consequences

| Failure | Consequence | Recovery |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Can't open lid (wrong verb) | Engine hints: "The lid is heavy stone. Perhaps you could push or slide it." | Use PUSH, LIFT, or SLIDE |
| Open sarcophagus E first (empty) | Slight disappointment, but the mystery is compelling | Open other sarcophagi — all remain accessible |
| Don't READ the tome | Miss the major lore revelation of Level 1 | Tome is portable — can be read later, even in Level 2 |
| Light runs out in crypt | Must navigate by FEEL; can still open lids and feel contents | FEEL descriptions exist for all contents |
| Skip the crypt entirely | Miss all treasure and lore | No gameplay penalty; lore-only loss |

### Failure Reversibility

**No failure possible.** All sarcophagi are openable. All contents are permanent (no timer, no trap). The tome can be taken and read anywhere. This is Zarfian Polite: the player always knows where they stand (open lids = find things), and no irreversible mistakes are possible. The sarcophagus lids, once opened, stay open.

## Design Rationale

### Why This Puzzle?

**Research grounding:** Three research threads converge here:

1. **Obra Dinn deduction model (§2.3 [15][16]):** "Strip detective work to observation and logic." The effigies are visual clues. The inscriptions are textual clues. The player who examines before opening makes better predictions. This is pure deduction from observation — no inventory gates, no tool chains.

2. **Emily Short's narrative reward principle (§1.3 [7][8]):** "Puzzles as narrative rewards — solutions should unlock story, not just the next room." The crypt doesn't unlock a room or advance the critical path. It unlocks *understanding* — who built the manor, why, and what they were guarding. The tome is the most significant narrative object in Level 1.

3. **Escape room "surprise" pillar (§3.1 [23]):** "At least one 'wow' moment per experience." The empty sarcophagus (E) is the surprise — the player expects to find something in every coffin. Finding one empty, with scratch marks, raises more questions than answers. It's the mystery that propels the story forward.

**Progressive complexity:** The sarcophagus puzzle uses PUSH/LIFT/SLIDE instead of OPEN — new verb territory. This extends the player's interaction vocabulary. But the action is intuitive (heavy stone lid → push it) so it's not frustrating. Per The Witness model (Frink §2.1 [11]): introduce one new concept per puzzle.

**Reward calibration:** This puzzle sits at the end of Level 1's longest optional chain: move rug → trap door → cellar → storage cellar → deep cellar → altar ritual → crypt. That's 6+ prerequisite puzzles. The reward must be proportional: the tome (critical lore), the silver dagger (powerful tool), burial goods (wealth), and the mystery of the empty sarcophagus (narrative hook). High effort → high reward. Per Frink's cognitive science (§5.3 [32]): the brain's reward response scales with difficulty overcome.

### Level Boundary Consideration

**Must cross levels:**
- **Tome** — narrative object; must persist. Player should be able to re-read it in Level 2+.
- **Silver dagger** — may be important for Level 2 puzzles (silver as supernatural material). If it would trivialize a Level 2 combat/cutting puzzle, design a natural consumption (dagger breaks on hard material, silver corrodes in acid, etc.).
- **Burial jewelry/coins** — trade goods. Should persist for economic interactions in Level 2.

**Flag for CBG:** All crypt objects are optional finds. Players who skip the crypt won't have them. Level 2 puzzles should NOT require these objects — they're bonuses, not prerequisites.

## GOAP Analysis

### What GOAP Resolves
- "PUSH lid" → standard force action (no tool needed)
- "TAKE tome" / "TAKE dagger" → standard take action
- "READ tome" → standard read action
- "LIGHT candle-stub" → fire source chain (match/candle → light stub)

### What GOAP Cannot Resolve (The Puzzle)
- Which sarcophagus to open (player choice/deduction)
- That effigies correlate with contents (observation insight)
- The significance of the empty sarcophagus (narrative comprehension)
- That the tome is the most important find (prioritization based on lore interest)

### GOAP Depth Analysis
- Open sarcophagus: 1 step (PUSH lid)
- Light candle stub: 1-2 steps (apply fire source)
- No complex chains — this is an exploration puzzle, not a tool puzzle
